What is This?

You will create hand-crafted letters to use as subheadings on your portfolio pages. These letters have texture and character — they introduce the focus of each page.

How to use them: Small subheadings that introduce each section of your research. Think poetic, not descriptive. A short title or quote that captures your idea.

Two Ways to Use Your Letters

After creating your hand-crafted letters, you have two options for adding them to your portfolio:

🔤

Option A: Create a Font

Upload to Calligraphr

Upload your scanned template back to Calligraphr to automatically convert your letters into an installable font file.

✓ Best for:
  • Clean, consistent letters
  • Using the same letters many times
  • Easy resizing and editing
  • Drawn or painted letters
Pros
  • Type normally
  • Auto spacing
  • Reusable
Cons
  • Loses some texture
  • Letters vectorised
✂️

Option B: Cut Out in Photoshop

Keep raster texture

Select and copy individual letters from your scanned template, placing them manually in your portfolio document.

✓ Best for:
  • Heavy texture or imperfections
  • Collage letters
  • Stamps with ink variations
  • Unique, one-off titles
Pros
  • Full texture kept
  • Authentic look
  • More control
Cons
  • More time needed
  • Manual placement

🤔 Which Should I Choose?

Do you want to keep all the texture, ink variations, and imperfections in your letters?

HOMEWORK: Create Your Letters

Complete this before class. Bring your scanned template.

Steps

1
Download Template

Go to Calligraphr.com and download a template. Select only the letters you need for your subheadings.

2
Print Template

Print the template. Each letter has a box — your letters must fit inside these boxes.

3
Create Letters

Fill each box with a letter using your chosen method:

  • Stamp with printing set or foam
  • Draw or paint directly
  • Collage cut-out letters
  • Any physical technique
Important: If stamping, remember letters need to be backwards on your stamp!
4
Scan

Scan at 300 DPI. Save as JPEG or PNG. Bring to class.

5
Decide Your Method

Look at your scanned letters and decide which method you'll use in class:

  • Option A (Font): If your letters are clean and consistent
  • Option B (Photoshop): If you want to keep heavy texture

Choose Your Method

Option A: Create a Font with Calligraphr

Convert your hand-crafted letters into an installable font, then type your subheadings normally.

Steps for Option A

1
Upload Template to Calligraphr
  • Go to Calligraphr.com and log in
  • Click "My Fonts" → "Upload Template"
  • Select your scanned template image
  • Calligraphr will automatically extract each letter
2
Preview and Adjust
  • Check each letter has been detected correctly
  • Adjust baseline or sizing if needed
  • Preview how your font looks with sample text
3
Build and Download Font
  • Click "Build Font"
  • Download the .ttf or .otf file
  • Double-click the file to install on your computer
4
Use in Photoshop
  • Open your A3 sheet in Photoshop
  • Select the Type Tool T
  • Choose your new font from the font menu
  • Type your subheading text
  • Position and resize using Ctrl+T
5
Add References & Knowledge
  • Use your handwriting font for citations and notes
  • Add Harvard references for any sources shown
  • Include contextual analysis or explanations

💡 Tips for Option A

  • Calligraphr free tier allows 75 characters — usually enough for A-Z uppercase
  • If the font doesn't appear in Photoshop, restart Photoshop after installing
  • You can create multiple fonts for different styles

Option B: Cut Out Letters in Photoshop

Keep all the texture and character of your letters by selecting and placing them manually.

Steps for Option B

1
Open Both Files
  • Open your A3 sheet in Photoshop
  • Open your scanned letter template in a separate tab
2
Select a Letter
  • Go to your template tab
  • Use the Rectangular Marquee Tool M
  • Draw a selection around the letter you need
  • Copy with Ctrl+C
3
Paste and Position
  • Switch to your A3 document tab
  • Paste with Ctrl+V
  • Use Move Tool V to position
  • Repeat for each letter in your subheading
4
Remove White Background (Optional)

If you want to remove the white paper background around your letters:

  • Select the letter layer
  • Go to Select → Color Range
  • Click on the white background
  • Adjust Fuzziness slider
  • Click OK, then press Delete

Or use Blending Mode: Multiply on the layer to make white transparent.

5
Group and Resize
  • Select all your letter layers in the Layers panel
  • Group them with Ctrl+G
  • Use Free Transform Ctrl+T to resize the whole word
  • Hold Shift while dragging corners to keep proportions
6
Add References & Knowledge
  • Use your handwriting font for citations and notes
  • Add Harvard references for any sources shown
  • Include contextual analysis or explanations

💡 Tips for Option B

  • Keep letter layers organised — name them or group by word
  • Multiply blending mode is the quickest way to remove white backgrounds
  • Adjust letter spacing manually for the best look
  • You can rotate individual letters slightly for a more organic feel
